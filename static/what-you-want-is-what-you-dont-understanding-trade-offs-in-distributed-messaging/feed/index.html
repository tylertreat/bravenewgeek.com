]]> By: Paul Banks https://bravenewgeek.com/what-you-want-is-what-you-dont-understanding-trade-offs-in-distributed-messaging/#comment-2079 Mon, 24 Aug 2015 13:15:33 +0000 https://bravenewgeek.com/what-you-want-is-what-you-dont-understanding-trade-offs-in-distributed-messaging/#comment-2079 @night Storm’s ordering guarantees are a feature. Other alternatives like Apache Flink have even stronger ones.

The point being made here is that those guarantees are not unconditional, and even when they hold do not come for free.

There are trade-offs at every level and if you can recognise patterns that allow you to operate correctly with fewer guarantees you will in general perform better.

Derek Collison’s quote is a good one – the less you rely on other system’s guarantees the better since they will break down somewhere.

The engineering tradeoff is in balancing this truth with the fact that re-inventing the desirable properties of exactly-once delivery or strong data consistency (or an approximation of them) yourself on top of limited-guarantee components is often likely to be unnecessarily expensive in development cost too. Assuming of course that you _really_ do need them.

Some more thoughts on this: https://banksco.de/p/distributed-system-guarantees.html.
